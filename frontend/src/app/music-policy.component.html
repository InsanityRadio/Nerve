Music Policy

To comply with Ofcom rules, we need to ensure that content we play sticks to certain rules. These rules are explained.

  1. We operate with no bad language, 24/7, 365 days a year. Exceptions to this are rare.

    The general rule of thumb is if you wouldn't say it to a child or to your grandparents, you can't say it on air.

    'Mild' words (as defined by Ofcom) such as ass are exceptions to this rule.

  2. We are high quality.

    You must only upload music that comes directly from CD, record label, or a reputable online store.

    That means no YouTube rips. No exceptions.

  3. We are impartial.

    Of course, many songs have political roots, but some overly political songs may be unsuitable for airplay.

    For example, 'Liar Liar' could not be played during the general election period.

  4. We act in the public interest.

    Music should not explicitly condone drug use or anti social behaviour.

    If a local or national incident occurs, we stick wholly to the truth, and show our mellow side.

As a station, we legally are obliged to stick to these rules. Hence, any breaches of them will be taken very seriously and may result in upload privileges being taken away.

At the time of writing, Nerve only covers music uploads. There will be provisions in the future to upload sound-bites.
